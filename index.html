History

Exercise Iniochos is an annual multinational military exercise held in Greece, hosted by the Hellenic Air Force's 117 Combat Wing at the Andravida Air Base. The exercise aims to improve the coordination and interoperability between different air forces, with a focus on enhancing their ability to operate in complex and challenging environments. The exercise is organized by the Air Tactics Center, in cooperation with the Hellenic Air Force's Fighter Weapons School (FWS), which is responsible for planning and executing the exercise scenarios. The exercise involves various combat wings working together in different scenarios that simulate real-world combat situations.

The first Exercise was held in 2015 and has since grown to become one of the largest and most important military exercises in the region. The exercise provides an opportunity for military personnel to enhance their skills and work with professionals from different countries. The participating countries have the chance to improve their military capabilities, test their equipment, and learn from each other's experiences. Exercise Iniochos helps to promote regional security and cooperation by building mutual trust and understanding between different countries. The exercise is an opportunity for the participating countries to strengthen their relationships and enhance their ability to work together in complex and challenging environments.
